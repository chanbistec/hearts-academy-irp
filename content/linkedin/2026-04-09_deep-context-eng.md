AI isn't just a tool anymore – it's your new teammate. But who leads the team?

The agentic era demands more than just coding skills. It demands engineers who can lead AI, not just write code for it. This is where **context engineering** becomes crucial.

What is it? It's the art of architecting the environment, rules, and information an AI agent needs to perform specific tasks effectively. Think beyond prompts – it's about structuring data, defining goals, setting constraints, and designing interaction flows for autonomous systems.

Why does it matter now? As AI takes on more complex, open-ended tasks, the engineer's role shifts. You won't just be building features; you'll be building the *system* that allows AI to build features. This requires strategic thinking, understanding AI behavior, and ensuring reliable, high-quality outcomes.
